How the board works, how we price a deal, and why we never sell you the flight.

How does The Flight Lounge work?

We continuously track fares from your home airports, build a median price for every route, and surface the genuine drops. Each fare lands on your board with a plain verdict so you know it's actually cheap before you click. You book direct with the airline; we never touch your payment.

Why don't the good-price verdicts show yet?

We refuse to call a price "good" off a thin sample. We're banking fare history now — once a route has ~2 weeks, its verdict switches on automatically. Until then prices are real and live; we just won't pretend to judge them.

Do you sell the flights?

No — deliberately. We point you to the airline or a trusted booking site and you pay them directly, so there's no markup. It also means we're not a travel agent and hold no ATOL; we're a deal-finding club.

What's the "found it cheaper" engine?

Two things no other club does. Catchment: we price every airport near you and show the cheapest. Reposition: we price clever two-leg routes that beat the direct fare — always with the self-transfer risks flagged honestly.

Are the self-transfer routes safe?

They can save a lot, but they're separate tickets — so a missed connection isn't protected, you may need to recheck bags, and transit visa/ESTA rules can apply. We flag every one of these on the recommendation so you decide with the catch in full view.
