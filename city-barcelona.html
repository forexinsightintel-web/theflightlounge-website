When to fly

Barcelona is a year-round city, but the sweet spot for price and weather is late September to early November and again March to May. You dodge the July–August peak (both crowds and fares), and it's still warm enough for the beach in the shoulder. January and February carry the cheapest fares of all if you don't mind a cooler city break — think museums, long lunches and empty Gaudí queues.

Our board is tracking London–Barcelona fares four times a day. Midweek departures (Tuesday–Thursday) are reliably cheaper than Friday or Sunday, and booking six to ten weeks out tends to land the best price without gambling on a last-minute drop.

Getting there for less

Three airlines fight over this route — easyJet and Vueling from Gatwick, Ryanair from Stansted and Luton — which is exactly why it's cheap. From the north, Manchester and Birmingham both run direct too, usually a few pounds more. If you're flexible, set the "nearby airports" toggle on the search: our catchment view will price every airport within reach of you, not just the obvious one.

From El Prat airport, the Aerobús to Plaça Catalunya is about €6 and takes 35 minutes; the R2 Nord train and the L9 metro are cheaper still. Skip the taxi rank unless you're in a group.

Where to stay without overpaying

Base yourself in Eixample for wide streets, Gaudí on the doorstep and the best-value mid-range hotels, or Gràcia for a more local, neighbourhood feel with cheaper dinners. El Born and the Gothic Quarter are gorgeous but you pay for the postcard. Prices swing hugely with the events calendar — a big conference or match can double room rates overnight — so check dates before you commit.

Book the flight first, then the room. A cheap fare with an overpriced hotel isn't a cheap trip — search both before you commit.

Doing it on a budget

The best of Barcelona is free or nearly: the beach, Park Güell's outer areas, the Bunkers del Carmel view at sunset, the Boqueria market, and simply walking the Gothic Quarter. Book Sagrada Família tickets online in advance to skip both the queue and the tout mark-up. Eat where the menu isn't in five languages, and take the lunchtime menú del día — three courses for what a mains costs at dinner.
